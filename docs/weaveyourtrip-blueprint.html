Product Blueprint · 2026

WeaveYourTrip

An AI-powered end-to-end travel architect that learns your taste, optimises every trip for cost, handles visa paperwork, and delivers a day-by-day personalised itinerary.

Web First Wizard + Chat Hybrid Redirect → Booking Freemium + Affiliate Budget Backpackers · Family Planners
Core Vision

Not just a travel planner — a personalised trip architect that handles everything from visa paperwork to daily itineraries, while actively saving money through smart scheduling. It learns your travel taste over time, getting smarter with every trip — and it updates the plan live even after you've departed.

Revenue Model
5 Monetisation Layers
1

Affiliate Commissions — Day 1 Revenue

Every redirect earns. Booking.com (4–15%), Airbnb (5–15%), Expedia (3–12%), Viator/activities (8–15%), Skyscanner (0.5–20%). Travel insurance affiliates add another 8–10%. Zero friction — starts earning before you have paid users.

Starts at launch · No minimum user base needed
2

Freemium Subscription — Core SaaS Revenue

Free: 1 active trip, basic itinerary, standard recommendations.
Premium (€7–12/month): Taste profile learning, unlimited trips, collaborative mode, live updates, offline access, Trip Score™, visa intelligence. Freemium builds trust first — users upgrade once they see value.

Target: ~5–8% freemium conversion
3

eSIM Sales — High-Margin Product

Built-in eSIM purchasing at itinerary confirmation. Surfaced at exactly the right moment — "You're going to Japan, get connected instantly on arrival." High margin, solves a real pain, zero extra effort from the user.

Typically €8–25 per eSIM sale
4

B2B White Label — Phase 3+ Revenue

Once the planning engine is proven, license it to travel agencies and tourism boards under their own branding. Recurring SaaS fee. Small agencies would pay well for a branded version of WeaveYourTrip's AI engine with their logo.

€500–5,000/month per agency client
5

In-App Booking — Phase 4+ Revenue

Once volume is proven, negotiate direct contracts with hotels and airlines for higher margins than affiliate rates. In-app checkout removes friction and captures more of the transaction value. Requires significant trust and volume first.

Higher margin than affiliate · Requires scale
⚠️ What to Avoid Early

Data selling and in-app ads both kill user trust — especially in a planning app where users share passport details, travel dates, and family information. Build trust first, monetise deeper later.
